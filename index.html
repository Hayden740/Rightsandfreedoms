Acknowledgement of Country

Seaford Secondary College is built on Kaurna Land. We acknowledge and respect the history and custodianship of the Kaurna people and recognise the important contributions of past and present leaders in building the community.

We also pay our respects to all language groups throughout South Australia and their cultures, as well as to all Aboriginal Elders past and present.

Warning: These lessons contain film, voices and images of deceased persons.

Disclaimer: Some lessons contain historically accurate language now deemed inappropriate. When discussing First Nations Peoples please use: Aboriginal · Torres Strait Islander · First Nations Peoples · Indigenous Australian.

Your Lessons

Work through each lesson in order. Read the content, watch the videos, and type your answers. Download your answers as a Word document at the end of each lesson to submit to your teacher.

Lesson 1
The White Australia Policy
Explore the government policies designed to keep Australia racially homogeneous, including the Immigration Restriction Act 1901 and how these policies were eventually dismantled.
→
Lesson 2
Assimilation Policy & The Stolen Generations
Examine the devastating policy of assimilation and the forced removal of First Nations children from their families — one of the darkest chapters in Australian history.
→
Lesson 3
The Freedom Rides & Wave Hill Walk-Off
Discover how 30 university students sparked a civil rights movement, and how the Gurindji people's seven-year strike changed Australian land rights forever.
→
Lesson 4
The 1967 Referendum & Land Rights
Examine the decade-long campaign for Aboriginal citizenship, the landmark 1967 vote, the Mabo decision, and the ongoing fight for recognition of traditional land ownership.
→
Lesson 5
The Apology & Contemporary Issues
Analyse Prime Minister Kevin Rudd's National Apology to the Stolen Generations, explore the 2023 Voice Referendum, and examine the ongoing effort to Close the Gap.
→
