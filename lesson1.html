The White Australia Policy

What is the White Australia Policy?

This term refers to a collection of Government policies introduced during the 1800s to mid-1900s to keep Australia 'white' — or mostly of European descent.

Sir John Forrest, WA Premier who supported the policy at the 1898 Federation Convention
"March of the Great White Policy" — propaganda poster of the era
"It is of no use to shut our eyes to the fact that there is a great feeling all over Australia against the introduction of coloured persons. It goes without saying that we do not like to talk about it, but it is so." — Sir John Forrest, WA Premier, 1898 Australasian Federation Convention
"We can bring in, without delay, our kinsmen from Britain and, if the numbers be insufficient, such other white races as will assimilate with our own. Or we can…see the doors of our house forced, and streams of people from the lands where there is hardly standing room pour in and submerge us." — Edmund Barton, Prime Minister of Australia, 1902

The Immigration Restriction Act 1901

  • The government limited immigration to Australia by excluding all non-Europeans and deporting any already here.
  • The Act also prohibited other "undesirables" — people with infectious diseases, recently imprisoned, or those deemed "idiots" or "insane."
  • Dictation tests were given to exclude any person who could not write a passage of 50 words in a European language chosen by the officer.
  • This Act was officially in place until 1958.
FederationA group of countries/states joined together under one government.
ImmigrationThe action of coming to live permanently in a foreign country.
RestrictionA limiting condition or legal measure.
DeportedExpelled from a country, often on the grounds of illegal status.
ProhibitedForbidden; banned.
DictationSaying words aloud for another person to write down.

The Assimilation Policy & The Stolen Generations

Mr Auber Neville was Chief Protector of Aborigines (1915–1936) and Commissioner of Native Affairs until 1940. He strongly believed in the permanent segregation of full-blooded Aborigines and recommended "breeding out" Aboriginal genes by reproducing with white people — a process he called assimilation.

SegregationThe enforced separation of different racial groups in a country, community or establishment.
AssimilationThe process of absorbing one group into another dominant culture, often forcibly.
"Are we going to have a population of one million blacks...or are we going to merge them into our white community and eventually forget that there were ever any Aborigines?" — Neville at the Native Welfare Conference, 1937
"This Conference believes that the destiny of the natives of aboriginal origin, but not of full blood, lies in their ultimate absorption by the people of the Commonwealth, and it therefore recommends that all efforts be directed to that end...The policy of the Commonwealth is to do everything possible to convert the half-caste into a white citizen." — Native Welfare Conference, 1937

From the 1890s to the 1970s, the Australian Government used the law to remove First Nations children from their families. These children became known as the Stolen Generations.

"Assimilation relied on the well-established and widely-accepted view that we were inferior to white Australians, that our way of life, our culture and our languages were substandard... Embedded within the policy of assimilation was a clear expectation of the cultural extinction of Indigenous peoples." — Professor Michael Dodson, Leading Indigenous Academic
"I grew up feeling alone, a black girl in a white world, and I resented them for trying to make me white but they couldn't wash away thousands of years of dreaming." — Aunty Rhonda Collard, member of the Stolen Generations
"We were each handed a pair of pyjamas with a number Mr Borland, the manager, had given us earlier printed on the pocket, and a shirt and pair of shorts also. I was number 33. Not Bill. Not even Simon. Just number 33." — Bill Simon, taken away aged 10
